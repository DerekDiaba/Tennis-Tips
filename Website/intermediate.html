Intermediate Tennis Tips

Take your game to the next level with these intermediate techniques. Focus on enhancing your volleying, serving, and positioning skills.

Serving

Mastering your serve can give you a significant advantage. Here’s how to refine your serving technique:

  • Grip: Use the Continental grip to enable better control and spin. Hold the racket as if you are shaking hands with it.
  • Stance: Position your feet shoulder-width apart with your front foot slightly forward. Balance your weight evenly.
  • Toss: Toss the ball slightly in front of you and high enough to allow a full extension of your arm during the serve.
  • Motion: Rotate your shoulders and hips as you swing. Aim to make contact with the ball at its peak and follow through smoothly.
  • Placement: Aim for various spots in the service box to keep your opponent guessing. Practice serving to different areas for greater accuracy.

Positioning

Effective positioning on the court can greatly enhance your performance. Follow these tips:

  • Baseline Position: Stay a few feet behind the baseline to give yourself enough time to react to the ball. Be ready to move forward or backward as needed.
  • Net Position: When approaching the net, position yourself closer to it and stay low. Be prepared to react quickly to volleys and passing shots.
  • Side-to-Side Movement: Use quick side steps to cover the width of the court. Position yourself to intercept the ball early and reduce your opponent’s options.
  • Anticipation: Read your opponent’s body language and shot selection to anticipate their next move. Position yourself accordingly to gain the upper hand.
